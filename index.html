№ 04 · Why Monthly
When every edit is a $200 invoice, sites go stale. Flat plans make improvement frictionless.

Add up what a small-business site really costs to keep alive: hosting, security, backups, monitoring, and a developer on call when something breaks. Bought separately, that stack runs more than $99–$249 a month — and you're the one coordinating it.

Here's the five-year math, named head-on: five years on a monthly plan can cost more than one big build. It also buys five years of edits, improvements, and a site that never rots. Most one-time builds are stale by year two, and every fix after that is a new invoice.

And the plans hold no hostages. I keep clients with results, not contracts.

You own your site, your domain, your content. Every monthly plan cancels with 30 days' notice — and after the 12-month $0-down term, the site is 100% yours. If you leave, you leave with everything.
