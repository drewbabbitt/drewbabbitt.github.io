№ 04 · Fair Questions

Fair questions

Run entirely by Drew Babbitt. Nothing outsourced, nothing offshored — you work with the person who builds it.

Why monthly plans instead of one big project fee?

Because a website or automation isn't done at launch — it's done when it's producing. Monthly plans keep me accountable for results every single month, keep your costs predictable, and mean you never get a surprise $4,000 “redesign” invoice three years from now.

Am I locked into a contract?

No. Every monthly plan cancels with 30 days' notice. The only exception is the $0-down site build, which carries a 12-month term because I'm financing the build for you. If you cancel a care plan, your site is yours — I'll hand over everything cleanly.

What do I actually own?

Everything. You own your site, your domain, your content. Every monthly plan cancels with 30 days' notice — and after the 12-month $0-down term, the site is 100% yours. If you leave, you leave with everything.

Who actually does the work?

I do. Drew B.-IT is deliberately a one-person studio backed by automation — that's why the prices look like this. You get senior-level work and talk directly to the person doing it.

Is the social automation safe for my accounts?

Yes — that's a hard rule here. Everything is built on Meta's official APIs and approved tools. Gray-area bots get accounts suspended; I won't build them, even if you ask nicely.

We already pay for Claude. Why do we need you?

Subscriptions don't create results — adoption does. I make sure your team has the right setup, the right prompts for your actual work, and a habit of using them. Clients typically see the plan pay for itself in saved hours within the first month.
