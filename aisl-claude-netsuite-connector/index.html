01

What it sees & does

governed by role: ClaudeMCP

In scope today

Sales Orders Quotes Invoices Purchase Orders Item Fulfillments Item Receipts
  • Look up any of the above — by number, customer, item, date, or status.
  • Query across records with saved searches and SuiteQL — "all open SOs past ship date," "top items this quarter."
  • Create and update those records — draft a quote, enter an order, note a receipt — with a human approving in the chat.
  • Summarize and report — turn raw transactions into digests, exception lists, and answers.

Out of reach

  • Payroll, HR, and employee records
  • Banking, payments & company financial setup
  • User accounts, roles & admin settings
  • Anything not granted to the ClaudeMCP role
The scope is a dial, not a switch. Any record type can be flipped to view-only, removed, or added by editing one role — no reinstall, no re-setup.

03

The 10X ERP migration workbench

most useful exactly when systems are in motion
Before cutoverData-quality sweeps

Query for duplicates, incomplete records, and stale open transactions so 10X imports clean data instead of inheriting years of clutter.

During mappingField & mapping validation

Pull exact SuiteQL extracts of what NetSuite holds — item by item, field by field — to verify what 10X says it imported against the source of truth.

Before it changesCurrent-state documentation

Interrogate real transactions to write down how order-to-cash actually works today — the runbook that usually lives only in people's heads walks out the door otherwise.

During parallel runReconciliation on demand

While both systems are live, ask for side-by-side checks — orders, balances, counts — and catch drift in minutes instead of at month-end.

ThroughoutAnswers without the old-system expert

Every "how did we handle X in NetSuite?" gets answered from the data itself — institutional knowledge stays queryable for as long as the account exists.

The window is the point. A migration is when questions multiply and the people who can answer them are busiest. This connector keeps the old system legible while the new one lands — and it took zero custom development to switch on.
